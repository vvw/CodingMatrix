Basic operations with vectors

Vector addition and translation

Vector addition

The addition of two vector is defined as the addition of the values in each corresponding position: $$\left[u_1, u_2, \ldots , u_n \right] + \left[v_1, v_2, \ldots , v_n \right] = \left[u_1 + v_1, u_2 + v_2, \ldots , u_n + v_n \right]$$

associatiity

$$(\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{y}) + \mathbf{z} = \mathbf{x} + (\mathbf{y} + \mathbf{z})$$

commutativity

$$\mathbf{x}+\mathbf{y} = \mathbf{y} + \mathbf{x}$$

Vector-scalar multiplication (scaling)

Vector multiplication

Multiplying a vector \(\mathbf{v}\) by a scalar \(\alpha\) is defined as multiplying each element of \(\mathbf{v}\) by \(\alpha\): $$\alpha \mathbf{v} = \alpha \left[ v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n \right] = \left[ \alpha v_1, \alpha v_2, \ldots, \alpha v_n \right]$$

For vector,

  • we refer to field elements as scalars,
  • we use them to scale vectors.

Dot-product: vector multiplication

Dot product

The dot product, or scalar product, is an algebraic operation that takes two equal-length vectors and returns a single number. Let \(\mathbf{u} = \left[ u_1, u_2, \ldots, u_n \right]\) and \(\mathbf{v} = \left[ v_1, v_2, \ldots, v_n \right]\), the dot product is: $$\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = \sum_{i = 1}^{n} u_i * v_i$$

Commutativity

$$\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = \mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{u}$$

Homogeneity

$$\left( \alpha \mathbf{u} \right) \cdot \mathbf{v} = \alpha \left( \mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{u} \right)$$

Distributivity

$$ \left( \mathbf{u_1} + \mathbf{u_2} \right) \cdot \mathbf{v} = \mathbf{u_1} \cdot \mathbf{v} + \mathbf{u_2} \cdot \mathbf{v}$$
