Convex and affine combinations

  • special cases of vector addition and by scalar multiplicatio
  • convex: used to obtain the line that joins two points (vectors)
  • affine: used to obtain the infinite line that cross two certain points

Convex convination

An expression of the form: $$\alpha \mathbf{u} + \beta \mathbf{v} \hspace{1cm} \text{where} \hspace{0.5cm} 0 \leq \alpha, \beta \leq 1, \, \, \alpha + \beta = 1$$ is called a convex combination of \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\).

The \(\mathbf{u}\)-to-\(\mathbf{v}\) line segment consists of the set of convex combinations of \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\)

Convex convination

An expression of the form: $$\alpha \mathbf{u} + \beta \mathbf{v} \hspace{1cm} \text{where} \hspace{0.5cm} \alpha + \beta = 1$$ is called an affine combination of \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\).

The line through \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\) consists of the set of affine combinations of \(\mathbf{u}\) and \(\mathbf{v}\)
